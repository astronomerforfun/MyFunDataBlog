Process Flow Cleaning your House? That easy...

November 9, 2018

First Look at Lucid

Objective:

I have been tasked with helping document processes for our company as we are begining the process of overhauling our systems/processes and incorporating an ERP system.

One of the tools I was introducted to for this project was Lucid https://www.lucidchart.com. I thought I would give my initial thoughts on the software thus far.

So what is it?

Lucid is a web process mapping software. You sign up, pay a subscription fee and you can use and publish as you see fit. There is also a Team Subscription that allows you and your group to collaborate. This would be great for undocumented procedures.

There are all sorts of flow charts, mind maps, etc. There are a ton of individual Shapes, fonts and my favorite (which has been a serious pain in every other mapping software I’ve used) the automatic fit of arrows. They just flow.

What does it do?

It helps with Process Mapping. It’s like Vizio but so much simpler to use (sorry Vizio). Within the first 30 minutes working with it I created a workflow for one of the processes I take part in every day. Moreover I was able to publish it to R Markdown (this blog was created in R Markdown) with the push of a button. See Below:


Overall Impressions:

I would like to learn more about how to create Process maps. It appears that there is a section on the website that helps with that so that’s cool. I learned a few tricks like copy and pasting blocks and dragging arrows (as opposed to creating new ones and getting the right size, etc) but I would like to learn more. I assume with a few flow charts I’ll have it down.

Regarding ease of use. Couldn’t be easier. It’s great. They offer a 7 day free trial so give it a whirl. Even cleaning my house might get its own flow chart? It’s that easy and quick to create.

I will follow up in 30 days to see where I’m at. But my first hour long exposure has been extremely positive.
